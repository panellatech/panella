# Alias and domain governance

Aliases are deterministic routing surfaces, so they must remain conventionally
single-purpose. Broad account and personal-label language is not an alias just
because it often appears near one concept. The recorded revocations of
`username` and `nickname` preserve that rule; their historical deterministic
bindings are recorded as approved remaps in the retention ledger.

`hr_lexicon` is intentionally separate from aliases. It contributes risk
evidence and cannot create a deterministic registry hit.

The YAML governance log is authoritative for its machine-checked delta. This
note explains the policy but does not authorise routing changes.
